Overview

Designed an RFID-enabled cognitive self-training system that lets four group-housed mice access an automated training enclosure individually — cutting training timelines from 5–6 months down to 10–15 days. RC Plane Structures Sub-Lead

ASME UCSD Student Design Project · Fall 2025 – Spring 2026

  • RoleStructures Sub-Lead
  • TimelineFall 2025 – Spring 2026
  • TeamASME UCSD Design Team
  • FocusStructural CAD & Fabrication

Overview

A remote-controlled aircraft built for the ASME student design initiative at UCSD, exposing the team to aerodynamics, structural design, software integration, and embedded data collection. A key feature is an onboard particle sensor that detects airborne particles — dust, smoke, and pollen — via laser light scattering, transmitting real-time data to a ground station for post-flight air-quality analysis. My role was to develop and refine the aircraft's structure, translating the team's conceptual ideas into a fabrication-ready model.

Key Contributions

  • Led structural design in Autodesk Fusion around the flight controller, battery, and onboard particle sensor placement.
  • Designed a 3D-printed front fuselage housing with internal cross-section frames to ensure steady airflow for accurate sensor data while maintaining rigidity at minimum weight.
  • Specified a lightweight carbon-fiber-rod rear fuselage to support the tail assembly and control surfaces.
  • Reduced structural weight by approximately 15% while maintaining reliability, then completed flight testing.
  • Presented design progress to industry professionals at the CAPS Industry Showcase at UC San Diego.

Automated CNT Sprayer for Electrode Coating

NanoBioElectronics (NBE) Lab, UC San Diego · 2024 – Present

  • RoleVolunteer Researcher
  • Timeline2024 – Present
  • LabNBE Lab, UC San Diego
  • FocusResearch & Automation

Overview

Designed and fabricated an automated spray system for depositing carbon nanotubes onto gold electrodes with high precision and repeatability. The setup integrated a solenoid-actuated spray gun, programmable time relay, laser alignment system, and a custom-built support frame positioned near a heated plate. This eliminated inconsistencies in manual spraying, improving the reliability of electrochemical characterization and supporting the lab's research on minimizing sensor variability for wearable and nanoscale biosensors.

Key Contributions

  • Built the automated sprayer — solenoid-actuated spray gun, programmable time relay, laser alignment, and heated-plate curing.
  • Developed MATLAB scripts to automate experimental data analysis, replacing a slow, manual Excel-based workflow with immediate plotting and visualization.
  • Designed and fabricated a jaw-motion mimicking device to validate salivary cortisol sensor readings in mouthguard devices under real-use conditions.
