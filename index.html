RFID-Based Automated Mouse Cognitive Training System

Senior Capstone (MAE 156B) · Team 13 · Fall 2025 – Spring 2026

Designed an RFID-enabled cognitive self-training system that lets four group-housed mice access an automated training enclosure individually — cutting training timelines from 5–6 months down to 10–15 days. Three RFID reader modules paired with an ESP32 actuate a dual-gate airlock, achieving a 10 cm read range, 100% detection success across all trials, and 400 ms tag-to-gate latency.

This was a full team capstone with its own dedicated website, including the complete report, design details, and results.

2-DOF Rectilinear System Modeling & PID Control

Lab Class Project (MAE 171A) · Spring 2026

Characterized a 2-DOF mass-spring-damper system by extracting dynamic parameters from experimental step responses and deriving a transfer function for simulation and control design. Performed sensitivity analysis under varying input amplitudes and mass configurations to evaluate PID controller robustness and identify performance limits. Processed and averaged multi-run experimental data in MATLAB, generating figures comparing simulated versus measured system responses.

Lattice Structure Design & Tensile Characterization of PMMA

Lab Class Project · Spring 2026

Designed two custom microlattice PMMA specimens at 70% effective void fraction and performed tensile testing to characterize their effective elastic modulus and ultimate tensile strength. Ran FEA simulations in Autodesk Fusion 360 to predict stress concentrations — and fracture locations confirmed the predicted high-stress regions in both designs. Applied digital image correlation to experimentally measure strain fields during loading and compared the results against analytical and FEA predictions.
